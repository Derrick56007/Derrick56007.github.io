Problem

Learning the choreography of dance is difficult.

It requires memorization of movement sets that are in time with the music.
Often times, audio players donâ€™t allow users to loop certain sections of the audio or change the playback rate. Users also struggle with memorizing some a certain part of the choreography.

Competitive Audit

Without our application, users will need to go on online video-sharing platforms, mainly Youtube, to learn their dance choreography.


Youtube & Vimeo

  • allows its users to control the pace of the video they are watching
  • did not offer a feature for the users to loop a certain part of the song
  • user will need to keep going back on their device during their dance practice and repeat a part of a choreography manually

Looper

  • one of the inspirations for our application
  • allows their users to loop a certain part of a video, which is one of the features we wish to offer in our application
  • does not allow their users to manage the speed of the video itself

We offer both of those features in one application, so the user wouldn't need to move from one application to another in order to achieve the final results they want.

Functional Prototype

Based on several ideas from our storyboards, we then designed a paper prototype to illustrate the significant & interactive elements that we hope to achieve in our application.

User Testing

Users were asked to perform several tasks to explore the full function of our app.

To get users feedbacks, we execute two ways of user testing - in person & online A/B testing. In both testings, users were asked to do some tasks that reflects the core functionality of our application. We then take their suggestions into account. In terms of A/B testing, we use Google Analytics to determine which design of the home page that gives users a better browsing experience.


Final Product

Dance Notes allows users to add their own custom cues, such as another audio track over the music that can help them in any way they want, such as adding their own counts to the music or give reminders at certain times in the audio. There are also text and image cues that users can add that will show on screen. In addition, users have the ability to choose which sections of audio to loop as well as change the playback rate.
